John von Neumann

The father of modern computing

Here's a time line of John von Neumann's life:

  • 1903 - Born in Budapest, Austria-Hungary
  • 1913 - Enrolled in the Lutheran Gymnasium in Budapest
  • 1921 - Earned a degree in chemical engineering at the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology Zurich
  • 1922 - Studied mathematics with David Hilbert and Hermann Weyl at the University of GÃ¶ttingen
  • 1926 - Received his doctorate in mathematics from the University of Budapest
  • 1928 - Invented the concept of the self-replicating machine, which laid the theoretical foundation for modern computing
  • 1930 - Began working at Princeton University
  • 1943 - Worked on the Manhattan Project, which led to the development of the first atomic bombs
  • 1945 - Co-authored the First Draft of a Report on the EDVAC, which described the design of the first stored-program computer
  • 1947 - Co-authored the report, "The Monte Carlo Method"
  • 1950 - Co-authored the book, "Theory of Games and Economic Behavior", which laid the foundation for game theory
  • 1955 - Awarded the Enrico Fermi Award for his contributions to the development of nuclear energy
  • 1956 - Co-founded the John von Neumann Theory Prize
  • 1957 - Died of cancer at the age of 53

John von Neumann's life and achievement are testimony to the far-reaching contribution that one man's towering intellect, persistence and scientific vision can make to human knowledge and progress.
